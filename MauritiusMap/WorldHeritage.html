Mauritius

UNESCO World Heritage Sites

Mauritius holds a unique place in human history. Its two World Heritage properties are deeply intertwined with the global narratives of slavery, indenture, resistance, and the forging of modern multicultural societies.

Aapravasi Ghat

Inscribed 2006

The "Great Experiment"

Located in the district of Port Louis, Aapravasi Ghat sits on the bay where the modern history of Mauritius began. Following the abolition of slavery in the British Empire in 1834, the British government selected Mauritius for the "Great Experiment."

This site was an immigration depot intended to demonstrate that "free" labor could replace slave labor. Between 1849 and 1923, nearly half a million indentured laborers, primarily from India, passed through these gates to work on the island's sugar estates.

Today, it stands as a testament to the indenture experience and the ancestors of the majority of the current Mauritian population.

  • Significance: The global launchpad for the indentured labor system that later spread across the world.
  • What remains: About 15% of the original depot, including the immigration shed, gatekeeper's hut, and the iconic 14 steps.

Le Morne Cultural Landscape

Inscribed 2008

A Citadel of Resistance

Le Morne Brabant is a rugged mountain on the southwestern tip of Mauritius. During the 18th and early 19th centuries, this imposing monolith became a fortress for runaway enslaved people, known as "maroons."

Its almost inaccessible cliffs and dense wooded slopes provided ideal shelter. Maroons established small settlements in caves and on the summit, living in freedom despite the harsh conditions. It became the physical embodiment of the fight against the inhumane system of slavery.

It is celebrated today not just for its dramatic physical beauty, but as a universal symbol of resistance, freedom, and the enduring human spirit.

  • Significance: A powerful global symbol of the resistance to slavery (Maroonage).
  • Geography: A basaltic monolith rising 556 meters above sea level, surrounded by a lagoon.
